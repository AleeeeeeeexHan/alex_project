  <a href="/travelguide/culture/">Chinese Culture</a> <a href="/travelguide/chinese-food/">Chinese Food</a>   Top 8 Chinese Dishes</div><h1>
      The 8 Most Popular Chinese Dishes You Should Try    </h1></div>
  <div id="MainhotelContent" class="row">
  <div class="col-md-5 col-sm-5 hidden-xs">
  	    <div id="sideBar">
                <ul class="leftNav">
            <li class="mainTitle">
			            <a href="/travelguide/chinese-food/">
						Chinese Food                        </a>
                        </li>
            <li><a href="/travelguide/chinese-food/chinese-food-type.htm">Chinese Food Types/Ingredients</a></li><li><a href="/travelguide/chinese-food/regional-cuisines.htm">Regional Cuisines</a></li><li><a href="/travelguide/chinese-food/eating-and-drinking-in-china.htm">Eating and Drinking</a></li><li><a href="/travelguide/chinese-food/dining-culture.htm">Chinese Eating Culture</a></li><li><a href="/travelguide/chinese-food/cooking.htm">Chinese Cooking Class</a></li><li><a href="/travelguide/menu/">Chinese Food Menus</a></li><li class="active">Top 8 Chinese Dishes</li><li><a href="/travelguide/chinese-food/famous-restaurants.htm">Famous Chinese Restaurants</a></li><li><a href="/travelguide/chinese-food/food-articles.htm">China Food &amp; Drink Articles</a></li><li><a href="/travelguide/chinese-food/chinese-food-facts.htm">Chinese Food Facts</a></li>          </ul>
           <div class="left_nav_bottom"><p>
	<a href="https://www.chinahighlights.com/redirect.asp?url=https://www.chinahighlights.com/tour/cht-fd-01/&amp;t=78002" target="_blank" rel="nofollow"><img src="//images.chinahighlights.com/allpicture/2015/11/7658ca10f8f74a729fc7e5bd.jpg" alt="chinese food ad" width="213" height="404"></a>

                <div class="photoBlock300 autowidht">
	<img class="img-responsive" src="//images.chinahighlights.com/2014/02/03d4e018844c4c768012bdd7_cp_301x224.jpg" alt="cooking" width="301" height="225"> Learn to cook Chinese dishes with China Highlights
</div>
<p>
	China is rich in tourist attractions. <strong>Delicious foods</strong> are an attraction indispensable in a good tour. With a long history, unique features, numerous styles and exquisite cooking, Chinese cuisine is one important constituent part of Chinese culture. <strong>Chinese dishes</strong> are famous for color, aroma, taste, meaning and appearance.
</p>
<p>
	The following are the eight most <strong>popular dishes among foreigners and Chinese</strong>. For customers&#x2019; convenience, we also list their Chinese character names and English pronunciations. These eight dishes are sweet and sour pork (chicken), gong bao chicken, ma po tofu, wontons, dumplings, spring rolls, chow mein and Peking duck. These dishes are available in most large restaurants in China.
</p>
<div class="row">
	<div class="col-md-8 col-sm-8 col-xs-24">
		<div id="chinesefood">
			<div class="foodlist">
				<img class="img-responsive" src="//images.chinahighlights.com/allpicture/2015/12/6934bb3107654e8195ad2396.jpg" alt="sweet and sour pork" width="250" height="200"> 
				<h2>
					Sweet and Sour Pork &#x7CD6;&#x918B;&#x91CC;&#x810A;
				</h2>
				<p>
					Sweet and sour pork has a bright orange-red color, and a delicious sweet and sour taste.
				</p>
				<p>
					At the very beginning there was only sweet and sour pork, but to meet demands, there have been some developments on this dish. Now, the pork can be substituted by other ingredients like chicken, beef or pork ribs. Read the DIY recipes of sweet and sour pork ribs. See <a href="/travelguide/chinese-food/cooking/sweet-sour-pork.htm">How to Cook Sweet and Sour Pork</a>.
				</p>
			</div>
		</div>
	</div>
	<div class="col-md-8 col-sm-8 col-xs-24">
		<div id="chinesefood">
			<div class="foodlist">
				<img class="img-responsive" src="//images.chinahighlights.com/allpicture/2015/12/967a1b2eec904d008fad2e3c.jpg" alt="kung pao chicken" width="250" height="200"> 
				<h2>
					Gong Bao Chicken &#x5BAB;&#x4FDD;&#x9E21;&#x4E01;
				</h2>
				<p>
					This is a famous Sichuan-style specialty, popular with both Chinese and foreigners. The major ingredients are diced chicken, dried chili, and fried peanuts.
				</p>
				<p>
					People in Western countries have created a Western-style gong bao chicken, for which the diced chicken is covered with cornstarch, and vegetables, sweet and sour sauce and mashed garlic are added. See <a href="/travelguide/chinese-food/cooking/kung-pao-chicken.htm">How to cook Gong Bao Chicken</a>.
				</p>
			</div>
		</div>
	</div>
	<div class="col-md-8 col-sm-8 col-xs-24">
		<div id="chinesefood">
			<div class="foodlist">
				<img class="img-responsive" src="//images.chinahighlights.com/allpicture/2015/12/5703d387681b439abe46d4aa.jpg" alt="ma po tofu" width="250" height="200"> 
				<h2>
					Ma Po Tofu &#x9EBB;&#x5A46;&#x8C46;&#x8150;
				</h2>
				<p>
					Ma po tofu is one of the most famous dishes in Chuan Cuisine with a history of more than 100 years. Ma (&#x9EBB;) describes a spicy and hot taste which comes from pepper powder, one kind of condiment usually used in Chuan Cuisine.
				</p>
				<p>
					The milky tofu is enriched with brownish red ground beef and chopped green onion. It is really a tasty delicacy. Read the DIY recipes of ma po tofu. See <a href="/travelguide/chinese-food/cooking/mapo-tofu.htm">How to Cook Ma Po Tofu</a>.
				</p>
			</div>
		</div>
	</div>
	<div class="clearfix">
	</div>
	<div class="col-md-8 col-sm-8 col-xs-24">
		<div id="chinesefood">
			<div class="foodlist">
				<img class="img-responsive" src="//images.chinahighlights.com/allpicture/2015/12/b340857a0dcd4842af86d983.jpg" alt="wonton" width="250" height="200"> 
				<h2>
					Wontons &#x9984;&#x9968;
				</h2>
				<p>
					Since the Tang Dynasty (618&#x2013;907), it has been a custom for people to eat wontons on the winter solstice.
				</p>
				<p>
					The most versatile shape of a wonton is simple a right triangle, similar to Italian tortellini. Wontons are commonly boiled and served in soup or sometimes deep-fried. The filling of wontons can be minced pork or diced shrimp. See <a href="/travelguide/chinese-food/wonton.htm">How to Cook Wontons</a>.
				</p>
			</div>
		</div>
	</div>
	<div class="col-md-8 col-sm-8 col-xs-24">
		<div id="chinesefood">
			<div class="foodlist">
				<img class="img-responsive" src="//images.chinahighlights.com/allpicture/2015/12/deb748fa174a4e8faf974229.jpg" alt="dumplings" width="250" height="200"> 
				<h2>
					Dumplings &#x997A;&#x5B50;
				</h2>
				<p>
					With a long history of more than 1,800 years, dumplings are a traditional food widely popular in North China. Dumplings consist of minced meat and chopped vegetables wrapped into a thin piece of dough skin.
				</p>
				<p>
					Popular fillings are mince pork, diced shrimp, ground chicken, beef, and vegetables. They can be cooked by boiling, steaming, or frying. Dumplings are a traditional dish eaten on Chinese New Year&#x2019;s Eve. See <a href="/travelguide/chinese-food/cooking/dumplings.htm">How to Make Chinese Dumplings</a>.
				</p>
			</div>
		</div>
	</div>
	<div class="col-md-8 col-sm-8 col-xs-24">
		<div id="chinesefood">
			<div class="foodlist">
				<img class="img-responsive" src="//images.chinahighlights.com/allpicture/2015/12/4187908b827b4be3b271be69.jpg" alt="chow mein" width="250" height="200"> 
				<h2>
					Chow Mein &#x7092;&#x9762;
				</h2>
				<p>
					The &quot;Chow mein&quot; is the Cantonese pronunciation of the Chinese characters above, which means stir-fried noodles. Generally speaking, this stir-fried dish consists of noodles, meat (usually chicken, beef, shrimp, or pork), onions and celery.
				</p>
				<p>
					For making chow mein, the noodles need to be cooked in boiling water for a while. After they becoming cool, then move to the step of stir-frying. See <a href="/travelguide/chinese-food/cooking/chow-mein.htm">How to Cook Chow Mein</a>.
				</p>
			</div>
		</div>
	</div>
	<div class="clearfix">
	</div>
	<div class="col-md-8 col-sm-8 col-xs-24">
		<div id="chinesefood">
			<div class="foodlist">
				<img class="img-responsive" src="//images.chinahighlights.com/allpicture/2015/12/67e1731499ad4afd8644f089.jpg" alt="peking roasted duck" width="250" height="200"> 
				<h2>
					Peking Roasted Duck &#x5317;&#x4EAC;&#x70E4;&#x9E2D;
				</h2>
				<p>
					Peking duck is a famous dish from Beijing, enjoying world fame, and considered as one of China&#x2019;s national dishes.
				</p>
				<p>
					Peking duck is savored for its thin and crispy skin. The Sliced Peking duck is often eaten with pancakes, sweet bean sauce, or soy with mashed garlic. It is a must-taste dish in Beijing! Read more about <a href="/beijing/food/beijing-duck.htm">Peking Roasted Duck</a>.
				</p>
			</div>
		</div>
	</div>
	<div class="col-md-8 col-sm-8 col-xs-24">
		<div id="chinesefood">
			<div class="foodlist">
				<img class="img-responsive" src="//images.chinahighlights.com/allpicture/2015/12/df8a52a2875f41af87bc4824.jpg" alt="spring rolls" width="250" height="200"> 
				<h2>
					Spring Rolls &#x6625;&#x5377;
				</h2>
				<p>
					Spring rolls are a Cantonese dim sum of cylindrical shape. The filling of spring rolls could be vegetables or meat, and the taste could be either sweet or savory. After fillings are wrapped in spring roll wrappers, the next step is frying. Then the spring rolls are given their golden yellow color.
				</p>
				<p>
					It is a dish especially popular in Jiangxi, Jiangsu, Shanghai, Fujian, Guangzhou, Shenzhen, Hong Kong, etc. Read more about <a href="/travelguide/chinese-food/spring-rolls.htm">Spring Rolls</a>.
				</p>
			</div>
		</div>
	</div>
</div>
